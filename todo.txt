The strength of the aspect is influenced by how close it is to being exact, with tighter orbs seen as stronger and more influential. Different orb sizes are used depending on the aspect, the planets involved, and sometimes the branch of astrology being practiced. Here’s a general guideline on orb sizes:

### Major Aspects
- Conjunction (0° aspect): Orbs of up to 8 degrees are common, though some astrologers might use tighter orbs for less influential planets.
- Opposition (180° aspect) and Trine (120° aspect): Orbs up to 8 degrees are generally acceptable.
- Square (90° aspect): A standard orb is up to 8 degrees.
- Sextile (60° aspect): Orbs are often a bit tighter, generally up to 6 degrees.

### Minor Aspects
- Quincunx or Inconjunct (150° aspect): Orbs are typically around 2 to 3 degrees.
- Semi-sextile (30° aspect): Similar to the quincunx, a 1 to 3-degree orb is common.
- Semi-square (45° aspect) and Sesquiquadrate (135° aspect): These aspects usually have orbs of about 2 to 3 degrees.

### Special Considerations
- Personal Planets (Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars): Tighter orbs are often used since their influence is more immediate and personal.
- Outer Planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, Pluto): Wider orbs can be used due to their slower movement and broader generational impact.
- Sun and Moon: Aspects to the Sun and Moon might be given wider orbs, reflecting their importance in the chart.
- Stelliums and Significant Configurations: In the case of stelliums (three or more planets in conjunction) or significant configurations like Grand Trines or T-Squares, astrologers might be more flexible with orbs to interpret the collective influence of the planets involved.

### Contextual Usage
- Natal Astrology: Tighter orbs emphasize aspects that are most significant in an individual’s personality and life events.
- Synastry: Slightly wider orbs can be used to account for the interaction between two charts, focusing on connections that define the relationship.
- Transits and Progressions: Orbs for transits might be tighter as they pinpoint specific times when influences are felt. For progressions, a tighter orb emphasizes the slow and significant development over time.

The use of orbs is somewhat subjective and can vary among astrologers. Some may adjust orb sizes based on their experience and the context of the reading, focusing on what they find to be most relevant and impactful.
